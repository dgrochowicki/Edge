## Match Results and Review

### NiP vs K27

**Final result:** NiP 2:0 K27  
**Map scores:**
- Ancient 13:8
- Nuke 13:8

NiP advanced to the upper-bracket semifinal. K27 dropped to the lower bracket.

#### Pre-match decision

- NiP moneyline: PASS
- Over 2.5 maps at 2.00: PASS

#### Review

The over correctly failed.

The NiP moneyline pass requires more nuance. NiP won convincingly, but a winning favourite does not automatically mean the available price offered value.

The exact NiP moneyline price must be preserved in future reports before judging the quality of this PASS.

### HEROIC vs 3DMAX

**Market:** Over 2.5 maps  
**Odds:** 1.95  
**Result:** Won

The match reached 1:1 after two maps, which settled the over as a winner.

#### Review

This was the strongest market read of the day.

The analysis favoured the map total over the match winner because the matchup looked competitive enough for both teams to take a map.

This is a useful hypothesis, not yet a proven pattern:

> Balanced BO3 matches may offer better value in the Over 2.5 maps market than in the moneyline market.

This must be tested over a larger sample.

### Wildcard vs Gentle Mates

**Market:** Wildcard moneyline  
**Odds:** 1.65  
**Final result:** Wildcard lost 0:2

#### Pre-match thesis

Wildcard was expected to benefit from Gentle Mates using a stand-in.

#### Review

The analysis may have overestimated the practical impact of the roster change.

A stand-in should not automatically be treated as a major negative. Future analysis should consider:

- the stand-in's individual level,
- role compatibility,
- time spent practising with the team,
- map-pool impact,
- whether the market already priced the roster change,
- the underlying level of the opponent.

This was the main analytical error of the day.

### paiN vs Phantom

**Final result:** paiN lost 1:2

#### Pre-match decision

- paiN moneyline at 1.36: PASS
- Over 2.5 maps: PASS / paper consideration

#### Review

The moneyline PASS was justified.

The short price did not adequately compensate for the risk, and the favourite lost outright.

The over would have won, but it was not selected as a real bet.

## Daily Lessons

- Do not treat a stand-in as automatic value against that team.
- Short favourites can still carry substantial downside.
- A PASS can be a successful decision even when the favourite wins.
- Do not evaluate decision quality using the final score alone.
- Market-specific hypotheses must be tracked, not assumed after one result.
- Record exact bookmaker odds for every reviewed market, including PASS decisions.
